Key Takeaway

The data reveals a significant shift in homeowner behavior driven by mortgage rate dynamics. Homeowners who locked in rates around 4% in 2021 are reluctant to relocate when current rates hover between 6-7%. This has led to fewer total projects but higher spending per project.

"Last year we saw less spending on more projects - people were taking care of deferred maintenance waiting to see if interest rates would drop. This year we realized rates would not drop back to the 4% range."

Ann Arbor's Resilience

While national construction spending decreased by approximately 0.5%, Ann Arbor's market showed 5.5% growth. This resilience can be attributed to the jobs and opportunity that the universities and their economic activity bring to the region.
